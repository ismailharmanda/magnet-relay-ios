# Flux Relay Design Baseline

## Visual Direction

Premium sci-fi lab, not cartoon toy. The board should read instantly: emissive grid tiles, glass/metal blocks, colored magnetic polarity, clear targets, and restrained instrument-panel UI.

## Motion Direction

Every action needs juice without hiding the puzzle state:

- App launch starts with the Flux Relay logo on a near-black lab surface, then hands off through cyan/amber/violet field sweeps into the home screen without a visible blank gap.
- Field charge-up before a pulse.
- Blocks accelerate and overshoot softly, then snap cleanly.
- Targets lock with a brief glow cascade.
- Level completion should feel like a lab handoff: success emblem, glass-panel congratulations, countdown rail, and a cyan scanline/wipe into the next round.
- Subtle camera shake and haptics on strong contact.
- Reduced motion should preserve clarity with shorter fades.

## Layout

Portrait iPhone first. Keep the board dominant, HUD compact, and bottom actions reachable by thumb. Text should never cover the board.

## App Icon Direction

Premium abstract Polarity Lens: a central charged glass-metal block suspended inside cyan magnetic field bands, with restrained amber/violet polarity accents on a near-black lab instrument surface. The icon must stay text-free, high-contrast, readable at small sizes, and avoid baked iOS rounded corners.

## Future Art Pack

Owned sci-fi lab set: tiles, magnets, blocks, target sockets, barriers, emitters, hazards, particle textures, icon, splash composition, and App Store screenshot-safe marketing stills.
